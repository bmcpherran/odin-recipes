Cauliflower Crust Pizza

Description

This cauliflower crust pizza is a creative twist on the classic pizza, featuring a gluten-free and low-carb crust made from cauliflower instead of traditional dough. This innovative pizza offers a lighter and healthier alternative to traditional pizza crusts, while still delivering all the delicious flavors of a traditional pizza. Topped with marinara sauce, cheese, and your favorite pizza toppings, cauliflower crust pizza is a satisfying and guilt-free option for pizza lovers looking to indulge in their favorite comfort food without compromising on taste or nutrition.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head cauliflower (about 4 cups riced cauliflower)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup almond flour or other gluten-free flour
  • Spices and seasonings (1 teaspoon garlic powder, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, 1/2 teaspoon salt)
  • Pizza toppings (1/2 cup marinara sauce, 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, 1/2 cup pepperoni slices, 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms, 1/2 cup sliced bell peppers, 1/4 cup sliced onions, 1/4 cup sliced olives)
  • Optional garnishes: Fresh basil leaves, red pepper flakes, grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet or pizza stone with parchment paper.
  2. Cut the cauliflower into florets and pulse in a food processor until it resembles rice. Microwave the riced cauliflower for 5 minutes, then let it cool.
  3. Place the cooled cauliflower in a clean kitchen towel and squeeze out as much moisture as possible.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cauliflower, 2 eggs or flaxseed eggs, 1/2 cup almond flour, 1 teaspoon garlic powder, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Mix until well combined.
  5. Transfer the cauliflower mixture onto the prepared baking sheet or pizza stone. Use your hands to press it into a thin crust shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  6. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until golden and crispy around the edges.
  7. Once the crust is cooked, remove it from the oven and top with marinara sauce, shredded mozzarella cheese, and your favorite pizza toppings.
  8. Return the pizza to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Remove the pizza from the oven, garnish with fresh basil leaves, red pepper flakes, and grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Slice and serve hot. Enjoy your homemade cauliflower crust pizza!
